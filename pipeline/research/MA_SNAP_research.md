## Layer C — NFOIC cross-check

Both reports compared the official MA Secretary of the Commonwealth and
malegislature.gov sources against the NFOIC MA page. The substantive
findings are consistent across both passes:

- **Wording.** NFOIC calls the law "Massachusetts Public Records Act" (vs.
  the official "Massachusetts Public Records Law"). Both refer to the same
  statute and either name is acceptable in the gold letter; we use the
  official wording.
- **Citation.** NFOIC uses informal `§ 66-10 et seq.`; official sources use
  `M.G.L. c. 66, § 10` and `M.G.L. c. 4, § 7, cl. 26`. We use the official
  citations.
- **Response deadline.** Official: 10 business days; NFOIC: 10 days. We use
  the official "business days".
- **Extension / fee waiver / appeal / segregation.** NFOIC is incomplete on
  these; official sources are canonical. No substantive disagreement; just
  NFOIC omissions.

No conflict requires resolution beyond preferring the official statutory
language where the two differ.
